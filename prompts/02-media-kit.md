## Instructions

## The Prompt

```
You are a professional publicist who designs media kits for expert podcast guests. Your media kits are host-friendly, visually structured, and answer every question a host has before they ask it.

Create a complete one-page media kit for the following expert:

**EXPERT PROFILE:**
- Full Name: [NAME]
- Title: [TITLE, e.g., "Executive Leadership Coach | Former VP of IT | Speaker"]
- One-Line Value Proposition: [What you help people do, e.g., "I help remote tech teams ship faster without burning out"]
- Headshot: [URL or describe: "Professional headshot, dark background, business casual"]
- Website: [URL]
- Social handles: [LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram — whichever are active]

**BIOS:**
- Short bio (what you'll use as source): [3-4 sentences covering your background, expertise, and results]

**CREDENTIALS (Top 3-5):**
- [e.g., "15 years leading IT teams at companies from 50 to 5,000 people"]
- [e.g., "Former VP of IT at Accenture subsidiary"]
- [e.g., "Built and sold a 12-person MSP in Las Vegas"]
- [etc.]

**PAST PODCAST APPEARANCES (or media):**
- [Podcast name + host + episode title, OR "First-time podcast guest — available press/social proof:"]
- [If first-time: link to LinkedIn, speaking video, YouTube, blog, press mention, etc.]

**5 SIGNATURE TOPICS (for episode ideas):**
Topic 1: [Title] — [2-sentence description of what you'd cover and who benefits]
Topic 2: [Title] — [2-sentence description]
Topic 3: [Title] — [2-sentence description]
Topic 4: [Title] — [2-sentence description]
Topic 5: [Title] — [2-sentence description]

**AUDIENCE FIT:**
- Who your ideal listener is: [e.g., "IT managers, operations leaders, and remote-first founders"]
- Why they'll love this conversation: [1-2 sentences]
- One listener takeaway that's immediately actionable: [1 sentence]

**TECHNICAL SETUP:**
- Microphone: [e.g., "Blue Yeti, studio quality"]
- Camera: [e.g., "Logitech Brio 4K"]
- Recording environment: [e.g., "Dedicated home studio, soundproofed"]
- Timezone: [e.g., "Pacific Time (Las Vegas, NV)"]
- Preferred recording software: [e.g., "Zoom, Riverside, Squadcast — all fine"]
- Episode length flexibility: [e.g., "30 to 90 minutes"]

**CONTACT & BOOKING:**
- Best email: [EMAIL]
- Calendar link (if available): [URL or "Reply to request availability"]
- Response time: [e.g., "Within 24 hours on business days"]

---

Generate a complete one-page media kit with the following sections:

**SECTION 1: HEADER**
- Name, title, one-line value prop
- Headshot placement note (where the image goes)
- Website + primary social handle

**SECTION 2: BIO IN 3 LENGTHS**
- 30-word intro bio (for live introductions)
- 75-word show notes bio (standard podcast use)
- 150-word full bio (website, media pages)

**SECTION 3: 5 SIGNATURE TOPIC CARDS**
For each topic:
- Episode title (punchy, searchable)
- Subtitle (who it's for)
- 4-5 bullet takeaways (what listeners will learn)
- One "hot take" or contrarian angle the host can tease in their intro

**SECTION 4: AUDIENCE FIT**
- 3-bullet list of who listens and why this conversation serves them
- One sentence: "Your audience will love this episode if..."
- One immediately actionable listener takeaway

**SECTION 5: SOCIAL PROOF**
- Past appearances table (show name / host / episode) — or "Upcoming first appearance — available references below"
- Press mentions or credibility links
- One pull quote or testimonial (if available)

**SECTION 6: TECHNICAL SPECS**
- Formatted as a quick-scan table: Mic / Camera / Environment / Software / Timezone / Availability

**SECTION 7: QUICK HOST FAQ**
Answer these 5 questions in 1-2 sentences each:
1. Can you provide questions in advance?
2. Will you promote the episode to your audience?
3. What's your cancellation/rescheduling policy?
4. Do you have a pre-interview call?
5. Can you record video AND audio?

**SECTION 8: CONTACT & BOOKING**
- Email, calendar link, response time
- One closing line that makes it easy to say yes

Format the entire output as structured plain text that can be copied into Google Docs or a Notion page and formatted. Use clear section headers, bullets where appropriate, and a professional tone throughout.
```

---

## Tips

- **Host your media kit** as a Google Doc (link sharing on) or a PDF on your website. Never attach a PDF to cold email — link to it instead.
- **Update it after every appearance** using Prompt 4's "media kit update" output.
- **Keep your bios current.** A stale credential (wrong title, old company) hurts credibility.
- **The 30-word bio is the one hosts actually use** when they introduce you live. Get this one exactly right.
