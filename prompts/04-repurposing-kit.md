# Prompt 4: Post-Interview Repurposing Kit

*Full skill — $29 one-time*

---

## Instructions

Run this after your episode goes live. One interview becomes 10 content assets — enough to fill your content calendar for a month.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a content strategist who specializes in repurposing podcast appearances into multi-platform content. Your job is to extract maximum value from a single interview so the guest's one recording becomes a month of content.

Generate a complete 10-asset repurposing kit for the following podcast appearance:

**EPISODE DETAILS:**
- Episode Title: [EPISODE TITLE]
- Podcast Name: [PODCAST NAME]
- Host Name: [HOST NAME]
- Publish Date: [DATE]
- Episode Link/URL: [URL]
- Episode Length: [e.g., "47 minutes"]

**KEY TAKEAWAYS (pull 3-5 from the actual episode):**
1. [TAKEAWAY 1 — specific insight, not generic]
2. [TAKEAWAY 2]
3. [TAKEAWAY 3]
4. [TAKEAWAY 4 — optional]
5. [TAKEAWAY 5 — optional]

**YOUR BEST QUOTE FROM THE EPISODE:**
"[PULL QUOTE — the most punchy, tweetable thing you said. Direct quote.]"

**GUEST PROFILE:**
- Name: [YOUR NAME]
- Title: [YOUR TITLE]
- Website: [URL]
- Primary social: [HANDLE — LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram]
- Email list / newsletter: [NAME or "none"]
- What you want people to do after finding you: [e.g., "book a free call", "download my guide", "follow on LinkedIn"]

**AUDIENCE:**
- Who listens to this podcast: [e.g., "remote engineering managers, 25-45, US/Canada"]
- Your audience (your followers/list): [e.g., "IT leaders and operations directors"]

---

Generate all 10 of the following assets:

---

**ASSET 1: LinkedIn Post (Share the Episode)**
- Hook line (first 2 lines — what shows before "see more")
- 3 key takeaways formatted as bullets
- Transition to CTA
- CTA with link to episode
- Total length: ~1,200 characters
- Tone: professional, conversational, first-person

---

**ASSET 2: Twitter/X Thread (7 tweets)**
- Tweet 1: Hook — the most surprising or contrarian thing from the episode
- Tweets 2-6: One insight per tweet — short, punchy, no jargon
- Tweet 7: CTA — "Full episode here: [URL] — follow [your handle] for more"
- Each tweet under 280 characters
- Use 1/ 2/ 3/ etc. numbering

---

**ASSET 3: Instagram Caption**
- Hook (first line — must make them stop scrolling)
- 3-4 insights from the episode, each 1-2 sentences
- Bridge to CTA ("Full episode link in bio")
- 15 relevant hashtags (mix: niche, topic, podcast-specific)
- Total length: 150-200 words

---

**ASSET 4: Email to Your List**
- Subject line (+ A/B variant)
- 200-word email body:
  * Personal opening (why you went on this podcast)
  * Top 2-3 things from the episode
  * Direct link with a reason to click now
- Tone: conversational, like emailing a friend

---

**ASSET 5: 60-Second Video Script (Talking Head)**
For a short-form video (Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn Video)
- Hook (first 5 seconds — pattern interrupt or bold claim)
- 3 points in 10-12 seconds each
- Close + CTA (5 seconds)
- Total: ~160 words
- Include [PAUSE] markers and [VISUAL CUE] notes for editing

---

**ASSET 6: Blog Post Opener**
Turn the episode into a standalone article intro:
- SEO headline (with target keyword in first 60 characters)
- 3-paragraph intro (200 words) that summarizes the episode's core insight as an article hook
- Suggested subheadings for the rest of the article (H2s, based on episode takeaways)
- Meta description (155 characters)

---

**ASSET 7: Guest Bio Blurb (for "Featured On" page)**
For your website's press/media page:
- 50-word bio that includes: [Name] appeared on [Podcast] discussing [Topic]. Key insight: [one sentence]. Episode link: [URL].
- Keyword-optimized (include your expertise + podcast name + result)
- Format: HTML-ready (use bold tags for name and podcast name)

---

**ASSET 8: Quote Card Text**
For a branded graphic (Canva, etc.):
- The pull quote from above, formatted for visual impact
- Suggested line breaks for how it would appear on a 1080×1080 image
- Attribution line: "— [Your Name], [Episode Title], [Podcast Name]"
- Suggested background color mood (based on your topic): [3 options]
- Optional: one alternate shorter quote pulled from the takeaways

---

**ASSET 9: Podcast Directory Description**
For your own website's "Podcast Appearances" or "As Seen On" page:
- Entry formatted as:
  [PODCAST NAME] | [DATE] | [EPISODE TITLE]
  [2-sentence summary of what you discussed and why it matters]
  [Link text: "Listen to Episode →"]
- Includes 2-3 keywords naturally embedded for SEO

---

**ASSET 10: Media Kit Update**
What to add to your media kit (from Prompt 2) now that this episode has aired:
- Updated "Past Appearances" table row: [Podcast Name] | [Host] | [Episode Title] | [Date] | [Link]
- One pull quote from the episode to add to your Social Proof section
- One key stat to highlight: [e.g., "Episode reached X downloads in first week" — placeholder if unknown]
- Suggested headline update for your media kit header (if the episode validates a new positioning angle)

---

Format all 10 assets clearly with numbered headers. Make each asset complete and ready to post with minimal editing — just fill in the link and hit publish.
```

---

## Publishing Schedule (Suggested)

| Day | Asset | Platform |
|-----|-------|----------|
| Episode drops | Email to list | Email |
| Day 1 | LinkedIn post | LinkedIn |
| Day 2 | Twitter/X thread | Twitter/X |
| Day 3 | Instagram caption + quote card | Instagram |
| Day 5 | Short video script (record + post) | Reels/TikTok/Shorts |
| Day 7 | Blog post (expand from opener) | Blog |
| Day 10 | Update "Featured On" page | Website |
| Day 14 | Update media kit | Internal |
| Day 21 | Repost LinkedIn quote card | LinkedIn |
| Day 30 | Newsletter mention (if relevant) | Email |

---

## Tips

- **The quote card (Asset 8) is your highest-engagement post.** Design it in Canva in 15 minutes and post as a standalone image.
- **The email (Asset 4) should go out the day the episode drops** — that's when your excitement is real and your subscribers can act immediately.
- **The blog post (Asset 6) builds SEO long-term.** Podcast guesting is a long game — the article drives organic traffic 6-18 months later.
- **Update your media kit immediately.** Every appearance you add makes the next pitch stronger.
