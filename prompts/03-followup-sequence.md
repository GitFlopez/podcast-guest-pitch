# Prompt 3: 3-Part Follow-Up Email Sequence

*Full skill — $29 one-time*

---

## Instructions

Most pitches get no reply in the first 72 hours — not because the host hates you, but because they're buried. This sequence adds value at each touch and closes professionally if there's still no response.

Run this after sending your pitch email. Schedule Email 1 for Day 3, Email 2 for Day 7, Email 3 for Day 14.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a professional podcast publicist writing a 3-part follow-up sequence for a guest whose initial pitch has not received a reply. The goal is to be helpful and persistent — never annoying, never desperate. Each email adds new value rather than just saying "checking in."

**CONTEXT:**
- Guest Name: [YOUR NAME]
- Guest Title/Expertise: [YOUR TITLE + NICHE]
- Podcast Name: [PODCAST NAME]
- Host Name (first name): [HOST FIRST NAME]
- Original Pitch Date: [DATE]
- Topic Pitched (best angle): [YOUR TOP TOPIC TITLE from Prompt 1]
- One new value-add or news hook since original pitch: [e.g., "was just featured in Forbes", "new data from my industry just dropped that's relevant to their audience", "just did an episode on X podcast that their audience follows", "just published a relevant article"]

---

Write 3 professional follow-up emails:

---

**EMAIL 1 — Day 3 (Short Bump)**
Goal: Friendly visibility bump. Add one new data point. Don't re-pitch everything.

Structure:
- Subject: Re: [original subject line] OR [Podcast Name] — one more thought
- 2-3 sentences max
- Reference the new value-add (the hook from above)
- One soft call to action: "Happy to share more if helpful" or "Let me know if a different angle would be a better fit"
- No attachment, no media kit link yet

Tone: Warm, low-pressure. Like a colleague following up, not a salesperson chasing a deal.

---

**EMAIL 2 — Day 7 (Social Proof Add)**
Goal: Add credibility without bragging. Open a door for a different angle.

Structure:
- Subject: [Guest Name] + [Podcast Name] — quick question
- Opening: brief, no "just following up" — something like "One more thought before I close the loop..."
- Drop one social proof element: name a recent podcast appearance, press mention, or relevant stat (e.g., "I just wrapped an episode on X podcast — 18K downloads in the first week")
- Offer pivot: "If the [original topic] angle doesn't fit right now, I also cover [alternate topic from Prompt 1] — happy to pitch that instead"
- CTA: "Totally fine if the timing isn't right — just let me know either way"

Tone: Confident but gracious. You're giving them an easy out AND an easy yes.

---

**EMAIL 3 — Day 14 (Graceful Close)**
Goal: Close the loop cleanly. Leave the door open for the future. No resentment.

Structure:
- Subject: Closing the loop — [Podcast Name]
- Opening: Acknowledge they're likely busy, no hard feelings
- One forward-looking hook: tie your topic to an upcoming seasonal event, trend, or date that makes it timely in 30-60 days ("If you're planning a [season] episode on [relevant theme]...")
- Clear close: "I won't keep filling your inbox — but if this ever feels like a fit, you know where to find me."
- Leave a resource: offer one free thing (a guide, a relevant article you wrote, a framework) — no strings attached
- Sign-off: warm, no pressure

Tone: Generous and professional. The goal is to be remembered fondly, not forgotten bitterly.

---

For all 3 emails:
- Keep total length under 150 words each
- Plain text format — no markdown in the actual emails
- Do not use phrases like "just checking in," "I wanted to follow up," or "per my last email"
- Each email should stand alone — hosts may not have read the previous ones
- Always include guest's full name, title, and website in the signature
```

## Scheduling Guide

| Email | Send Day | Send Time | Goal |
|-------|----------|-----------|------|
| Pitch (Prompt 1) | Day 0 | Tue–Thu, 8–10 AM host timezone | Initial outreach |
| Follow-up 1 | Day 3 | Same window | Bump with new hook |
| Follow-up 2 | Day 7 | Same window | Social proof + pivot |
| Follow-up 3 | Day 14 | Same window | Graceful close |

**After Day 14:** Move to a "revisit in 60 days" list. Seasons change, topics shift, and hosts often come back to pitches they meant to reply to.

---

## Tips

- **Don't use Gmail "schedule send" for all 3 at once.** Read the replies first — if they respond to Email 1, cancel the rest.
- **The graceful close (Email 3) is the most underrated.** Many hosts reply to Email 3 with "Sorry, buried! Yes, let's do this." The professional close triggers guilt-driven yeses.
- **Track your outreach** in a simple spreadsheet: Podcast Name / Host / Pitch Date / Follow-up 1 / Follow-up 2 / Follow-up 3 / Result.
