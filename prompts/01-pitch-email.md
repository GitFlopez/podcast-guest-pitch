## The Prompt

```
You are an expert podcast publicist who books A-list guests on leading shows. Your pitches are personalized, specific, and get hosts excited — not defensive.

Write a complete podcast guest pitch email for the following expert:

**GUEST PROFILE:**
- Name: [YOUR NAME]
- Title/Role: [YOUR TITLE, e.g., "Executive Leadership Coach" or "Founder of XYZ SaaS"]
- Expertise/Niche: [YOUR PRIMARY EXPERTISE, e.g., "remote team leadership for mid-market tech companies"]
- Top 3 Credentials: [LIST 3 — e.g., "15 years managing IT teams", "Former VP at Fortune 500", "Author of 'Lead Remotely'"]
- One concrete result you've achieved or helped a client achieve: [e.g., "Helped a 40-person remote team reduce Slack noise by 60% and ship 2x faster"]
- Any past podcast appearances or press: [LIST OR "First-time guest"]
- Your website: [URL]
- Your LinkedIn/social: [HANDLE OR URL]

**TARGET PODCAST:**
- Podcast Name: [PODCAST NAME]
- Host Name: [HOST FIRST NAME]
- Recent episode title you listened to: [EPISODE TITLE — be specific, don't fake this]
- What you liked about that episode: [1-2 sentences, genuine reaction]
- Estimated listener size or niche: [e.g., "~15K listeners, remote work professionals"]
- How your expertise fits their audience: [1 sentence]

**DESIRED OUTCOME:**
- Ideal episode length: [e.g., "30-45 minutes"]
- Recording format preference: [e.g., "Zoom video, audio-only fine too"]
- What you want listeners to do after: [e.g., "visit your website, download your free guide, follow on LinkedIn"]

---

Generate the following:

**1. THREE SUBJECT LINE VARIANTS**
- Variant A: Curiosity angle (makes them want to open)
- Variant B: Credibility angle (name-drops the result or credential)
- Variant C: Relevance angle (directly ties to their recent episode)

**2. PITCH EMAIL BODY** (under 300 words, no fluff)
Structure:
- Opening: Reference the specific episode they mentioned. One genuine observation. No fake flattery.
- The ask setup: One sentence bridge from their content to your expertise.
- 3 TOPIC PITCH ANGLES: Each with:
  * Working episode title
  * 3-bullet takeaway list (what listeners will learn)
  * Why this fits THEIR audience specifically
- Proof points paragraph: 2-3 sentences. Credentials + result. No bragging tone.
- Logistics block: 3 bullets — format flexibility, recording tools, pre-interview prep offer
- Soft CTA: 2 options — calendar link (if available) or "reply yes and I'll send times"
- P.S.: One social proof hook (a result, a stat, or a name-drop)

**3. FORMATTING NOTES**
- Plain text — no markdown in the actual email
- No subject line in the body
- Short paragraphs, max 3 sentences each
- Warm but professional — not salesy, not sycophantic

Output the 3 subject lines first, then the full email body.
```

---

## Tips

- **Always listen to a real episode** before pitching. Hosts can tell when you haven't.
- **Pick your best topic angle** from the 3 generated and lead with it in your subject line.
- **Send from a professional email** — not Gmail if you have a domain.
- **Personalize the opening** — the prompt generates a template, but tweak the episode reference to be specific to what you actually heard.
- **Don't attach your media kit to the first email.** Offer to send it if they're interested. Attachments kill deliverability.
