## Prompts

### Prompt 1 (Free Preview): Personalized Pitch Email
Generate a compelling, personalized pitch email with 3 topic angle options. Includes subject line variants, proof point framing, and a soft CTA that respects the host's time.

**Input:** Guest name, expertise/niche, 2-3 credentials, target podcast name + host name + recent episode title, desired outcome

**Output:**
- 3 subject line variants (curiosity / credibility / relevance angles)
- Personalized opening referencing the specific podcast episode
- 3 topic pitch angles (each with a working title + 3 bullet takeaways)
- Proof points paragraph (credentials without bragging)
- Logistics block (episode length flexibility, recording format, pre-interview prep offer)
- Soft CTA with 2 options (calendar link or reply-to-confirm)
- P.S. with social proof hook

---

### Prompt 2: One-Page Media Kit
Generate a polished, host-ready media kit that makes booking easy. Hosts receive dozens of pitches — this makes yours the one they actually read.

**Input:** Guest name + title, 3-sentence bio (short/medium/long), headshot URL or description, top 3 credentials, top 3 podcast appearances (or "first-time guest"), 5 talking points / episode topics, audience demographics, website + social handles, technical setup (mic/camera/quiet space), availability

**Output:**
- Header block (name, title, 1-line value prop, photo placement)
- Bio in 3 lengths: 30 words (intro), 75 words (show notes), 150 words (website/full)
- 5 signature topic cards (title + 3-5 bullet takeaways each)
- Audience fit section (who listens, why they'll love this guest)
- Social proof block (past appearances, media logos, stats)
- Technical specs block (recording format, timezone, turnaround)
- Quick FAQ (Can you provide questions in advance? Will you promote the episode? What's your cancellation policy?)
- Contact + booking block

---

### Prompt 3: Follow-Up Sequence (3 Emails)
Most pitches get no reply — not because they're bad, but because hosts are buried. This 3-part sequence closes the loop professionally without being annoying.

**Input:** Guest name, podcast name, host name, original pitch date, topic pitched, 1 new value-add or news hook

**Output:**
- **Email 1 (Day 3):** Short, friendly bump — adds one new data point or relevant angle not in original pitch. Subject line: [podcast name] — quick follow-up
- **Email 2 (Day 7):** Social proof add — name-drops a recent appearance or relevant press mention. Asks if a different topic angle would be a better fit.
- **Email 3 (Day 14):** Graceful close — "closing the loop" framing, leaves door open for future pitching, offers a specific future topic tied to a seasonal hook

---

### Prompt 4: Post-Interview Repurposing Kit
You did the interview — now squeeze every drop of value from it. Generates 10 content assets from a single podcast episode so one recording feeds your content calendar for a month.

**Input:** Episode title, podcast name, host name, publish date/link, 3-5 key takeaways from the episode, your bio/title, your website/social

**Output:**
- LinkedIn post (share the episode — hook + 3 takeaways + CTA, 1,300 chars)
- Twitter/X thread (7 tweets: hook, 5 insights, CTA with link)
- Instagram caption (150 words + 15 hashtags)
- Email to your list (subject line + 200-word email promoting episode)
- Short video script (60-second talking-head: "I was on X podcast and...")
- Blog intro paragraph (turn the episode into a standalone article opener)
- Guest bio blurb (for the show notes — 50 words, keyword-optimized)
- Quote card text (pull the single best quote for a branded graphic)
- Podcast directory description (for your own website's "Featured On" page)
- Pitch reuse asset (update your media kit with this appearance + key stat from episode)
